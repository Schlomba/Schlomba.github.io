Green Bay Community Center Launches Summer Food Drive

The Green Bay Community Center officially launched its annual Summer Food and Clothing Drive this morning. Organizers set up collection bins outside the main entrance, welcoming a stream of local residents looking to donate. The drive aims to support families struggling with rising grocery costs during the summer months.

An Urgent Need in the Neighborhood

Summer is often the hardest season for local food banks because donations drop significantly after the holidays. Volunteers are working extra hours to sort through incoming canned goods, boxed meals, and gently used summer clothing. Organizers hope to collect over 1,000 pounds of non-perishable goods by the end of the week.

"We are deeply moved by the immediate generosity of our neighbors today," said event coordinator Smitty Weberjagermanjansen. "Every single item donated stays right here to help people in our own backyard."

How You Can Get Involved

Donations will be accepted daily from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM until Sunday evening. The center is specifically requesting high-protein items like peanut butter, canned tuna, and beans. For more information on volunteering, citizens can read the full schedule published in the latest edition of the Local Community Chronicle newspaper.
